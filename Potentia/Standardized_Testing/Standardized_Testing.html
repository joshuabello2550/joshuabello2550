What is it?

Standardized tests are tests given uniformly to students in order to collect data about skill or readiness.There are three types of standardized tests that students take when applying to college: the ACT, the SAT, and the SAT Subject Tests. The ACT and SAT test students' ability to understand and present ideas and information and are used by most colleges to determine admission. The SAT Subject Tests, on the other hand, deal with specific knowledge on certain topics and are typically considered when applying to specific courses. It is important to know what and where your child wants to study to ensure that all required and recommended tests are taken. Another important test type is the AP Exam. These exams are on specific subjects and are complementary to AP courses, but can be self-studied for and taken without the class. More info on these exams can be found in its respective sections above.
