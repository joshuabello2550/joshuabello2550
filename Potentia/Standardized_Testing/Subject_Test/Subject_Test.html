What is it?

SAT Subject Tests are subject-based standardized tests separate from the SAT. Compared to the SAT, SAT Subject Tests are more specific to the subject the student chose. Subject Tests are not required by most schools but taking an SAT Subject Test and getting a high score can help to improve a student’s college application and boost their chances of being accepted into the colleges they apply to. Schools that do require students to take the SAT Subject Tests allow students to choose the tests the take or may require students to take specific test along with one of the student’s choosing. If students choose what tests they can choose out of a multitude of different types of subject tests. They are:

Math Subject Tests:
  • Math Level 1: college-prep-level algebra and geometry
  • Math Level 2: contains the same information tested in Math Level 1 test in more detail but also includes precalculus and trigonometry
Science Subject Tests:
  • Biology-E: (ecology) biological populations
  • Biology-M: (molecular) biological chemistry
  • Chemistry: evaluated a student’s knowledge on college-level chemistry
  • Physics: tests a student’s knowledge of major physics concepts
English Subject Tests:
  • Literature: tests a student’s understanding of major literary works throughout the English canon
History Subject Tests:
  • US History: tests students on pre-Columbian American history up to the present day
  • World History: focuses on societies ranging from prehistory to 1900 CE
World Language Subject Tests:
  • Korean, Latin, Modern Hebrew, Japanese, Chinese, Italian, French, Spanish, and German.
  • They test students on vocabulary, grammar structure, and reading comprehension.
