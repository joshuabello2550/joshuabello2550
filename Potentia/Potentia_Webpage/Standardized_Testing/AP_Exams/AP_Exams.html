What is it?

Advanced Placement, or AP, is a program run by the College Board that allows highschool students to experience classes on par with intro-level college courses. Students in AP have the opportunity to earn college credit, enabling them to take more advanced classes when they start college. College credit is only given to students based on if they pass the AP exam and if their college accepts that AP credit. The AP exam is scored on a scale of 1-to-5, on which 3 is considered passing. Taking AP classes can improve a student’s college application by showing that the AP class they took was something they cared about, as well as showing that they can manage the rigor involved with such a course.

How to Register

In order to register for AP classes students have to check with their highschool to see if the school offers AP classes the student wants to take or AP classes in general. This means that the registration process for AP classes differs from school to school. In some schools you have to pass a placement test to register for an AP class. If a students school does not offer AP classes they can still register for the AP exam but they would have to study on their own.

If a student attends a school that offers AP classes the teacher will normally talk to the students about registering for the test. If the teacher doesn’t talk to the students about registering for the AP exam, students can talk to their school counselor or their AP coordinator.

Below are the steps to registering for AP Exams:

  • Students enrolled in an AP course at their school should follow the below instructions
    1. Get your "Join Code" from your teacher
    2. Go to the College Board website's "AP Students" page
    3. Sign into or create a College Board account
    4. Once logged in, scroll down and click "Join a Course or Exam"
    5. Ensure that you're registering for the right course
    6. Enter any additional information requested
    7. Go to "Register for AP Exams" under the "Taking Exams" drop-down menu for conluding logistical instructions
  • Students that wish to take an exam for a course that they are not enrolled in can self-study and find a local admintrator of said exam. College Board explains how to accomplish this on a page titled "I’m homeschooled. How can I take an AP Exam?"
