What is it?

The ACT is the other of the two important standardized college admission tests in the United States. Similar to the SAT, the ACT is used to measure students’ readiness for college, and provide colleges with one common data point that can be used to compare all applicants. The ACT also has 4 sections, but they are divided as English, Math, Reading, and Science. The maximum score on the ACT is a 36, with the United States average being a 21. All colleges and universities that require standardized testing accept both the SAT and ACT scores but students should check the requirements of the different colleges or universities they are applying to.

How to Register

In order to register for the ACT you need to make an account or log onto the ACT website.

  1. Go to the ACT website's Registration page.
  2. Log into or create your account
    • US students creating an account should refer to the slides below
    • Non-US students will be sent to an alternate page. Click "Create an Account" and fill out the requested information
  3. Select "Register Now"
  4. Read everything carefully and fill out the subsequent forms
  5. Review and Register!
  6. Make a note or reminder of your testing time and location, as well as materials to bring (No. 2 pencil and Calculator)
