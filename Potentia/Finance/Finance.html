Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)

FASFA, what is it?

FAFSA is the Free Application for Federal Student Aid. FAFSA is used to apply for different types of federal student aid such as federal grants,scholarships, work-study opportunities, and general loans. Grants and scholarships do not need to be repaid but with a work-study job students receive paid, part-time work that is generally on campus. A loan is money a student has to repay, usually after they graduate. Just like in the name FAFSA is completed and submitted for free and is generally the largest source of financial aid which helps to pay for college. The FAFSA form submitted to the government can also be used by schools to determine a student’s eligibility for the school’s financial aid policy.

What is Financial Aid?

Financial Aid is money given to a student to help them pay for college. Different types of financial aid have different terms. Some types of financial aid need to be paid back, such as loans, some types need to be earned, such as work-study, and other types of financial aid are gifts that do not need to be paid back or earned, such as Pell Grants. The only way to receive financial aid is to apply.

There are multiple different sources of financial aid but the 4 main sources are:

  • Federal Government
  • Colleges and Universities
  • State Governments
  • And Private Organizations

In order to apply for financial aid many people use FAFSA.

What is Needed?

In order to fill out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), a student will need:

  • Their social security number
  • Their Alien Registration number (if they are not a citizen of the US)
  • Their federal income tax returns, W-2s, and other recorder of income earned.
    • A good way to transfer this information is by using the IRS Data Retrieval Tool.
  • Bank statements and records of investments (if applicable)
  • Records of untaxed income (if applicable)
  • And a FAFSA ID so the FAFSA form can electronically signed.

How to apply?

In order to apply go to https://studentaid.ed.gov/sa/.
