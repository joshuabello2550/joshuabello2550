POTENTIA

Joshua B, Madison D, Jalal G, Angelica S

Brainstorming

When we first started this project we initially came up with three ideas.

  • One of them was Bookworm which was a social media site that would help people overcome readers rut.
  • The other one was Their Potential which was a website that was initially geared towards immigrants and first-generation families in informing them about the college application process but later expanded to include any student that wanted a concise and central source of information on understanding how to navigate the college system.
  • Our last idea was to improve on FlowH2O and either attempt the ultrasonic flowmeter or improve on the web design.

After much debate and discussion, we decided to further analyze Bookworm and Potentia. We created a Business Ideation document for each product that outlined:

  • What is the problem the company is solving?
  • Who is this solving the problem for -- who’s the customer?
  • How is it solving the problem?
  • What is the business model -- how are they making money?
  • Who are some of your competitors and how do they solve this problem?
  • How will you be better or worse than your competitors?

Interviews

After we had outlined the two potential projects we pitched both ideas to the class and received feedback on what we could improve on and figuring out the details for each idea. We then set out to see if the problem we were trying to fix was truly a major issue or if people didn't have the problem to the extent we thought. We started by developing a list of interview questions for each topic then broke out into two groups and asked students and parents at CMU how they responded to our questions. Each group applied different techniques to gather information. Angela and Madison asked the questions and immediately typed up the responses as they talked. As for me, I recorded the conversation and later played it back to ensure that I didn't miss any details in our conservation.
